## About the Author

![Author overlooking the horizon](assets/page-51-image-1.png)


Shu Min, also known as Simon Shu Min, is an environmental planner, systems thinker, and sustainability practitioner.

For more than twenty years, he has continuously worked in fields related to environmental planning, landscape design, urban development, ecological restoration, regional governance, and sustainable development. He has participated in environmental, urban, energy, ecological, and regional development projects in both China and Australia.

His research interests have long focused on the relationship between environment and civilization, with particular attention to the interaction mechanisms among climate change, ecosystems, resource governance, artificial intelligence, and the future evolution of society. In recent years, he has continued to explore ClimateOS, Earth System Validation Science, the Computable Climate Zoning Planning System — CCZPS, Possibility Computing, and AI-assisted governance.

Unlike traditional technical research, his work places greater emphasis on cross-scale, interdisciplinary, and cross-civilizational perspectives. It attempts to build connections among environmental science, planning practice, systems science, and humanistic reflection, while exploring a new operating framework in which observation, cognition, validation, governance, and evolution are mutually coupled.

From the arid regions of northwestern China to the eastern coastal areas, from urban development to regional ecological governance, and from environmental practice to reflections on civilization, the author has always focused on one fundamental question:

How can humanity achieve long-term, stable, and creative development within the limits of the Earth’s environment?

*The Torch and the Horizon* is both a staged record of this exploration and an open starting point for continued thinking toward the future.
